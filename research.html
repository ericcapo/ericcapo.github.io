Research lines

Our research focuses on understanding aquatic microbial ecosystems through cutting-edge molecular ecology methods. We integrate environmental DNA, paleogenomics, multi-omics, phylogenomics to unravel microbial changes in past, current and future freshwater and marine ecosystems

Unravel microbial diversity in lake ecosystems

Lake microbial diversity
What microbes do know

We study the microbial diversity in lake surface waters and the active microbial layer in underlying sediments. We developed a drone-sampling approach to collect samples for a large number of lakes and provide the first catalogue of microbial diversity in Swedish lakes.

Methods: drone-enabled water sampling, sediment coring, metabarcoding, metagenomics

🌊 Microbial metabolisms in coastal systems
What microbes did in the past

Aquatic oxygen minimum zones are expanding due to warming and nutrient pollution. Our lab investigates how microbial communities — bacteria, archaea, and protists — respond to deoxygenation gradients. Using high-resolution time-series and multi-omics (metatranscriptomics + metabolomics), we decipher functional shifts, metabolic adaptations, and cascading effects on biogeochemical cycles (nitrogen, sulfur, carbon).

🌍 Approaches: water sampling, sediment coring, metabarcoding, metagenomics, genome-resolved metagenomics, genone-centric metatranscriptomics

Past microbial functions
From genes to ecosystem services

We use sedimentary ancient DNA (sedDNA) to reconstruct past changes in freshwater and marine microbial communities over centuries to millennia. By analyzing genetic records preserved in sediments, we uncover how microbial assemblages responded to climate shifts, eutrophication, deoxygenation and other environmental stressors. This long-term perspective is essential to predict future trajectories under ongoing anthropogenic pressures.

Approaches: sediment coring, ancient metagenomics, phylogenomics
