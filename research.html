Research lines

Unravel microbial diversity in lake ecosystems

We study the microbial diversity (bacteria, archaea, protists) in lake waters and the active microbial layer in underlying sediments. Using a novel drone‑based sampling approach, we collect water from hundreds of lakes across Sweden, combined with sediment coring. Together with metabarcoding and metagenomics, this effort is generating the first comprehensive catalogue of microbial diversity in Swedish lakes.

Methods: drone-enabled water sampling, sediment coring, metabarcoding, metagenomics.

Explore microbial metabolisms in coastal systems

We investigate how microbial communities respond to expanding oxygen minimum zones in coastal systems caused by warming and nutrient pollution. Using metagenomics and metatranscriptomics combined with oxygen‑gradient incubations, we track functional shifts, metabolic adaptations, and cascading effects on nitrogen, sulfur, and carbon cycling as well as methylmercury formation. Our goal is to predict how deoxygenation reshapes coastal biogeochemistry and to inform mitigation strategies for neurotoxin production in a warming ocean.

Methods: water sampling, sediment coring, metabarcoding, metagenomics, genome‑centric metatranscriptomics.

Track past changes in aquatic microbial functions

We use sedimentary DNA to reconstruct how freshwater and marine microbial communities changed over centuries to millennia. By analyzing genetic records preserved in sediment cores, we uncover how microbes responded to past climate shifts, eutrophication, deoxygenation, and other environmental stressors. This long‑term perspective helps us link past ecosystem states to present‑day dynamics and improve predictions of future trajectories under ongoing anthropogenic pressures.

Methods: sediment coring, ancient metagenomics, phylogenomics.
